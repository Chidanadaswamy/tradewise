### Top 10 Problems New Investors Face

1. **Information Overload & Jargon**: Overwhelmed by complex financial concepts and terminology (P/E ratio, EBITDA, options greeks).
2. **Noise vs. Signal**: Reliance on social media influencers, Reddit forums, or Telegram tips rather than objective analysis.
3. **Absence of Emotional Discipline**: Susceptibility to FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) during bull markets and panic-selling during corrections.
4. **Poor Risk Management**: Lack of stop-losses, improper position sizing, and over-concentration in single stocks/sectors.
5. **The Leverage Trap**: Trading complex derivatives (Futures & Options) with high leverage, leading to rapid capital wipeout.
6. **Unrealistic Expectations**: Viewing the stock market as a get-rich-quick scheme rather than a long-term wealth compounding engine.
7. **Intimidating or Trivialized UIs**: Professional brokers are too complex, whereas modern gamified brokers can trivialize trading risk.
8. **Lack of Performance Tracking**: Failing to maintain a trading journal to audit mistakes and review past trades.
9. **Transition Anxiety**: Emotional friction and fear when moving from zero-stakes virtual simulation to real money.
10. **High Onboarding Friction**: The barrier of broker selection, KYC, and funding an account before taking any action.

### Why They Lose Money

- **Buying High, Selling Low**: Trading reactively based on fear and greed.
- **Overtrading**: High trading frequency driven by boredom or "revenge trading" to recoup losses, leading to high fees.
- **Leveraged Derivatives**: Over 90% of retail options traders lose money because they are trading a leveraged, zero-sum game.
- **The "Psychological Gap"**: Virtual trading promotes rational choice, but real capital triggers irrational, emotional decision-making.
- **Institutional Disadvantage**: Competing against high-frequency algorithms (HFTs) and institutional resources.

### Existing Solutions

- **Simulators**: TradingView, Thinkorswim (paperMoney), Investopedia Simulator, Webull (focus: replicating execution).
- **Education**: Zerodha Varsity, Investopedia Academy, YouTube (focus: theoretical knowledge).
- **Social/Gamification**: StockGro, eToro, Public.com (focus: copy trading, competitions).
- **Analysis Tools**: Finviz, Screener.in, TradingView (focus: charting and filtering).

### Gaps in Current Solutions

1. **The "Monopoly Money" Effect**: Simulators lack real emotional weight, allowing users to build dangerous risk habits.
2. **Missing Transition Bridge**: No gradual pathway between a $100,000 demo account and micro-investing real capital.
3. **Speculation Over Education**: Gamified apps often incentivize high-frequency day-trading over healthy long-term investing.
4. **Disjointed User Journey**: Users must stitch together different platforms to learn, chart, analyze, and execute.
5. **No Behavioral Post-Mortems**: Current simulators show final account balances but fail to audit behavioral errors (e.g., "You hold losing positions too long and cut winners too early").
